Jack's Jiu-Jitsu

A brief summary of my combat sports experience

Jack's team

This is me with the Invictus Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu team. One day while I was at my computer (where every minute of my free time was spent) I decided to watch several old boxing matches for entertainment. One thing lead to another and I discovered the concept of grappling, and the combat sport style of Jiu-Jitsu. A few months later I found the courage to go to a local gym I had heard good things about on the Internet. I've now been at the Invictus gym for nearly two years training Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu and Muay Thai under coaches Rick, Ben, Ashley, Mindy, Fernando, and Sean, the first three of which are pictured in the photograph above.

Jack's guard

This is me employing a knee shield guard to prevent my friend Rafael from achieving a dominant position during Jiu-Jitsu training. Under Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu rules, lying on your back is completely acceptable. The grappler then proceeds to engage mainly with his or her legs. This is typically regarded as a defensive position, but a skilled player of this game can be as much or even more dangerous from the bottom of the fight than from the top while expending minimal energy. Over the course of my time training I've become known for taking this position and developed a reputation in my gym for having a dangerous leg triangle choke.

Jack failing to pass a guard

This is me being prevented from achieving a dominant position by my friend Jared, a much bigger man who is unused to grappling from a more defensive position due to his highschool wrestling background. The rules of collegiate-style wrestling do not allow a person to take up a position on their back, specifically pinned on both of their shoulders. I have the opposite problem of being allowed to lay on my back too often. As such, Jared will commonly go to his back immediately, forcing us both to play the game we know the least. We have mixed success.
